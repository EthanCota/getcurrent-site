Security

How we handle
your data

We build automation systems on your accounts — not ours. That means your customer data, your API keys, and your credentials stay under your control from day one. This page documents what we do, what we don't do, and what we're working toward. Honestly.

We are not SOC 2 certified. We are a new business working toward audit-readiness. What we have: a documented security baseline, a vendor risk register, and an approved-tool registry. We will tell you exactly where the gaps are if you ask.

1. Your credentials

During an engagement, you share API keys and admin logins so we can build and configure the system. Here is exactly how we handle them:

  • Stored in Bitwarden only. Every credential goes into a separate, 2FA-protected password manager vault (Bitwarden — SOC 2 Type II audited). Not in email, not in Slack, not in any document.
  • Used only for the work in scope. We access the systems listed in your Statement of Work and nothing else.
  • Deleted within 5 business days of project close. When the engagement ends, we revoke every key we held and send you written confirmation before we close the project. (MSA §8.2)

2. Your data stays in your accounts

We build on a BYOK (Bring Your Own Keys) model. Your workflow runs on your OpenAI organization account, your Twilio phone number, your Vapi or Retell account, and your hosting. Your customer data — call recordings, SMS content, form submissions, reservation data — flows through accounts you own and can audit, not through ours.

We do not aggregate, retain, or process client data on our own infrastructure after an engagement ends.

We do not sell, license, or share your data with any third party. Zero exceptions. This commitment is in writing in our Master Services Agreement §8.4.

3. AI tools and your data

We use AI services — primarily OpenAI GPT-4o and Anthropic Claude — in workflow builds and internal tooling. Our policy:

  • Any workflow that processes your data runs on your API keys on your account. Your OpenAI organization can opt out of training data use — we recommend this and will walk you through it.
  • Our internal tools (audit drafts, research) run on our own API accounts. We do not send client data through our internal AI accounts.
  • We do not use your data to train or fine-tune any model.

4. What we never accept

These data categories are hard stops regardless of engagement scope:

PHI regulated by HIPAA We do not accept HIPAA-covered work. MSA §4.5 states this explicitly. If a workflow would route protected health information, we decline the engagement.
Payment card and bank account numbers No reason for these to come to us. We advise clients to update billing on their own dashboard.
Social Security Numbers Your payroll system (Gusto, ADP) handles these, not us.

6. Device and access hygiene

  • Full-disk encryption (FileVault) on our primary work machine.
  • Separate browser profile for all client-related sessions — no commingling with personal accounts.
  • 2FA on every business account — Bitwarden, Google, GitHub, Cloudflare, and all vendor accounts. Authenticator app, not SMS, for critical accounts.
  • Local demo instance is shut down after each session. Credentials are not left in running containers.

7. Incident response

If we discover or suspect unauthorized access to a credential we held:

  1. Revoke the credential immediately — before investigating, not after.
  2. Disable any affected workflow.
  3. Notify you within 24 hours of scoping the incident, with a factual account of what happened, what we've already done, and what comes next.
  4. Document the root cause and fix it, not just the symptom.

We do not delay notification to avoid an awkward conversation.
