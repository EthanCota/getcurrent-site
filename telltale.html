Offshore-spend intelligence for AI automation consultants and small agencies.

What the offshore-replacement pitch looks like without this

You know your ICP: companies spending $500-1,200/month on offshore VAs for tasks a workflow could handle. Finding them takes hours of prospecting on LinkedIn and job boards. Getting to the task-level detail — what exactly they're paying the VA to do — requires a conversation that may or may not happen.

By the time you're in the room, you're asking discovery questions to find out what you could have known in advance. That's time, and it's a weaker pitch than walking in with a specific build estimate already drafted.

Apollo and ZoomInfo give you firmographics. They don't tell you which tasks a company's VA handles, which of those tasks are automatable, or what the displacement ROI looks like. That information is buried in public job posts — Telltale reads them for you.

How the data is produced

Telltale is not a database of claimed offshore spend. It's a methodology applied to public evidence.

  1. 1

    Mine public offshore job posts

    Collect job posts from platforms where offshore VA roles are advertised. Filter to SD-market companies and NAICS codes relevant to automation-replacement engagements.

  2. 2

    Decode task taxonomy from post language

    Parse each post into a structured task list. Job posts are written for human readers — the taxonomy work translates "manage inbox and respond to routine inquiries" into discrete, automatable task atoms.

  3. 3

    Score each task against the automation Atlas

    Each task atom is matched to a node in our business-process automation reference (490 process nodes scored by automation difficulty). The match produces an automation fit score and a build-complexity estimate.

  4. 4

    Calibrate with build data post-engagement

    After each client engagement sourced from the Telltale list, we log predicted vs. actual tasks, actual vs. estimated spend, and automation match rate. That feedback loop is what separates a calibrated product from a raw list.
