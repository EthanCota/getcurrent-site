Who this is for

AI automation consultants and small agencies who pitch offshore-replacement. You already know your ICP: companies spending $500–1,200/month on offshore VAs for tasks a workflow could handle. Finding them takes hours of LinkedIn and job board prospecting. Getting to the task-level detail — what exactly they’re paying the VA to do — requires a discovery call that may or may not happen.

Apollo and ZoomInfo give you firmographics. They don’t tell you which tasks a company’s VA handles, which of those tasks are automatable, or what the displacement ROI looks like. That information is buried in public job posts. This report reads them for you.

Not for you if: You’re looking for verified contact lists (this is an intelligence layer, not an enriched CRM export — see the honest scope note below). Not useful if you don’t have technical depth to execute API integrations; the leads are strongest for consultants who can actually build the workflows described.

Honest scope note

Contact coverage is partial. 22 of the 163 companies have email or site contact pages confirmed. 8 have domains verified but need a footer/LinkedIn lookup for a contact name. 31 need manual name resolution. This report tells you who to target and why — contact enrichment is a separate 5–7 day pass.

Sample is directional, not market-sizing. The 90-day window is OnlineJobs.ph-heavy. Vertical ratios are directional signals, not census data. The data tells you where to start, not the full market size.

Timing windows close. REsimpli is shipping native AI ISA features within approximately 12 months (per research as of June 2026). Real estate leads in this database are most valuable in the next 2 quarters. E-commerce leads are durable longer (Amazon SP-API has no single incumbent tool closing the gap yet).

Questions

Is this a verified contact list?

No. It’s an intelligence layer. 22 companies have confirmed emails or contact pages; the rest require enrichment work (LinkedIn, site footer lookup). The report tells you who to target and why — and which of the 22 to hit first. Contact coverage details are documented in Section 5 of the report.

Are these only San Diego companies?

No. The 163 companies are primarily US-based (CA, FL, TX prominent) with some AU/NZ/Canada. Only 3 companies cross-reference to San Diego. The database is useful for any consultant regardless of geography — most AI automation work is remote.

How recent is the data?

Harvested over a 90-day window ending June 2026. Posts from OnlineJobs.ph (387), RemoteOK (71), and Remotive (3). The timing windows documented in the report (e-commerce SP-API migration, real estate SaaS clock) are as of June 2026.

What’s the outreach pack exactly?

Three cold email templates built for specific verticals: (1) e-commerce / Amazon sellers using the SP-API migration as the opening, (2) the bookkeeping trio (invoice automation angle), (3) a general VA-replacement opener. Plus the scoring rubric that ranked all 163 companies, which you can adapt to your own prospecting. Plus a 15-minute async Q&A via email.

Do I need technical skills to use this?

The targeting intelligence is valuable to any consultant or agency pitching automation. But the leads are strongest for someone who can actually build the workflows described (n8n/Make + API integrations like SP-API, ServiceTitan, QBO). If your pitch is “I’ll set up your Zapier” rather than “I’ll build the API integration,” the technically-specific leads in this database may be out of scope for your current offering.
